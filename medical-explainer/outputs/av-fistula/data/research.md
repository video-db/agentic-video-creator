# AV Fistula (Arteriovenous Fistula) — Research Brief

## Definition
An arteriovenous (AV) fistula is an irregular connection between an artery and a vein. Normally, blood flows from arteries through capillaries to veins. With an AV fistula, blood flows directly from an artery into a vein, bypassing capillaries. This means tissues below the bypassed capillaries receive less blood supply.

## Two Main Contexts
1. **Pathological (acquired/congenital):** AV fistulas can occur naturally due to injury, congenital conditions, or genetic diseases. These may require treatment.
2. **Surgically created for dialysis:** In people with late-stage kidney failure, an AV fistula is surgically created (usually in the forearm) to provide reliable vascular access for hemodialysis. This is the preferred type of dialysis access.

## Causes
- **Injuries:** Gunshot or stab wounds where a vein and artery are side by side
- **Congenital:** Arteries and veins don't develop properly in the womb
- **Genetic conditions:** Osler-Weber-Rendu disease (hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasia) — causes pulmonary AV fistulas
- **Dialysis surgery:** Intentionally created in forearm for hemodialysis access

## Symptoms
- Small AV fistulas: often no symptoms, only need monitoring
- Large AV fistulas may cause:
  - Purplish, bulging veins (similar to varicose veins)
  - Swelling in arms or legs
  - Decreased blood pressure
  - Fatigue
  - Heart failure
- Pulmonary AV fistulas: cyanosis (pale/blue lips or fingernails), clubbing of fingertips, coughing up blood
- GI tract AV fistulas: gastrointestinal bleeding

## Risk Factors
- Older age
- Female sex
- Cardiac catheterization (especially in groin)
- Blood thinners (anticoagulants)
- High blood pressure
- Increased BMI

## Complications (if untreated)
- **Heart failure** (most serious) — increased blood flow strains the heart
- **Blood clots / DVT** — may lead to pulmonary embolism or stroke
- **Leg pain (claudication)** — blocked blood flow to muscles
- **Internal bleeding** — in stomach and intestines

## When to See a Doctor
If you have signs/symptoms of AV fistula, seek early detection to reduce risk of blood clots or heart failure.

## Treatment
- Monitoring (small fistulas)
- Compression
- Catheter-based procedures
- Surgery
